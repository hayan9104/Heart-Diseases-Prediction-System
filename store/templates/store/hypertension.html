About Hypertension

Hypertension, also known as high blood pressure, is a condition where the force of blood against the artery walls is consistently too high. It can lead to serious health issues such as heart disease, stroke, and kidney failure if left untreated.

Causes of Hypertension

  • Unhealthy diet, including high salt intake
  • Lack of physical activity
  • Excessive alcohol and tobacco use
  • Stress and genetic factors
  • Underlying conditions such as diabetes or kidney disease

Prevention and Management

While hypertension can be managed, adopting a healthy lifestyle is key to preventing it:

  • Maintain a balanced diet low in salt and high in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains
  • Engage in regular physical activity, such as 30 minutes of moderate exercise daily
  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol consumption
  • Manage stress through relaxation techniques like meditation
  • Regularly monitor your blood pressure
