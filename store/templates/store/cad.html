Understanding Coronary Artery Disease

What is Coronary Artery Disease?

Coronary Artery Disease (CAD) is a condition where the major blood vessels that supply your heart with blood, oxygen, and nutrients become damaged or diseased. Usually, this is caused by the buildup of plaque, which causes coronary arteries to narrow, limiting blood flow to the heart.

Key Facts:

  • Very common condition with more than 10 million cases per year in India
  • Can range from no symptoms to chest pain to heart attack
  • Requires medical diagnosis and often needs lab tests or imaging
  • While treatment can help, this condition cannot be cured
  • Chronic condition that can last for years or be lifelong

Common Causes:

  • Cholesterol-containing deposits (plaque) in your arteries
  • Inflammation in the arteries
  • Build-up of fatty materials that narrow or block your arteries

Treatment Approaches:

  • Lifestyle changes
  • Medication
  • Angioplasty
  • Surgery

Common Symptoms

Note: Coronary Artery Disease requires a medical diagnosis and can range from no symptoms to severe conditions like heart attack.

While some people may experience no symptoms at all, others may experience the following:

Pain Symptoms:

  • Chest pain (Angina)
  • Discomfort or pressure in the chest area

Gastrointestinal Symptoms:

  • Indigestion
  • Nausea

Whole Body Symptoms:

  • Light-headedness
  • Sweating (especially cold sweats)
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fast heart rate

Important: If you experience any of these symptoms, especially chest pain, seek medical attention immediately.

Emergency Response

If you suspect someone is having a heart attack, take these immediate actions:

  • Call emergency services (911) immediately
  • Have the person sit or lie down
  • Loosen any tight clothing
  • If prescribed, help them take nitroglycerin
  • If unconscious, begin CPR if you're trained
  • If available, use an automated external defibrillator (AED)

Remember: Time is crucial in heart emergencies. Don't wait - seek immediate medical attention!

Treatment Options

Treatment includes supportive care and lifestyle modification for symptomatic management. While this condition cannot be cured, several treatment approaches can help manage symptoms and improve quality of life:

Self-Care & Lifestyle Changes:

  • Quitting smoking
  • Weight loss
  • Regular physical exercise
  • Low fat diet

Medications:

  • Statin (for cholesterol management)
  • Anticoagulant (blood thinners)
  • Beta blockers
  • Antianginal medication
  • Calcium channel blockers

Medical Procedures:

  • Coronary stent placement
  • Angioplasty

Surgical Options:

  • Coronary artery bypass surgery

Note: Treatment plans are personalized and should be discussed with your healthcare provider.
